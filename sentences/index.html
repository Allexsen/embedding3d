    What is this?

    The cloud is passages from four sources (arXiv, Wikipedia, ELI5, social media), embedded with a sentence encoder and projected to 3D with UMAP. Tiers of 10K / 40K / 100K passages — default 40K, switchable top-right, along with the encoder (MiniLM/MPNet) and the embedding precision. Colors are sources — registers of English visibly cluster.

    Compare phrasings: your sentences are embedded in this browser and their nearest corpus passages light up. Same idea in different wording lands in different regions — that's the point. The 3D picture is illustrative; the metric cards (cosine, overlap, source mix) are computed in the full embedding space.

    Generate answers: each phrasing is sent to a small LLM several times; every sampled answer is embedded and placed as a ✦ star in its phrasing's color (corpus passages stay round dots, your phrasings are ◆ diamonds). Separated answer clouds mean the wording changed what the model says, not just what search retrieves.

    What gets downloaded

    · corpus tier: ~15–70 MB (on page load, per tier/precision)
    · embedding model: ~25–110 MB (auto, cached)
    · generation model: ~0.8 GB (only after you confirm on first Generate, cached)
    Everything runs client-side; API mode is the only thing that sends your prompts anywhere.
